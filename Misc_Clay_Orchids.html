Miscellaneous Orchids of clays
Calochilus and other Species

             
 
 
 
Copper Beard Orchid
Calochilus herbaceus
Albany - Auckland
Photo: Felicity Goodyear-Smith
Flowers October - December
Found in wet ground amongst scrub
north of Auckland. Greenish-yellow with
red stripes on the petals. Labellum has a
violet lamina surrounded by red whiskers.
Critically endangered.

 More Information
 
Beard Orchid
Calochilus herbaceus viride
Te Paki - Far North
Photo: Eric Scanlen
Flowers October - December
Structurally no different from
C herbaceus, but the red is absent
from the lateral petal stripes and from
the labellum beard. White replaces the
usual violet-purple labellum disc.

 More Information
 
Brown Beard Orchid
Calochilus paludosus
Rainbow Mountain - Waiotapu
Photos: Michael Pratt
Flowers October - December
Found on poor soils and pakihi. End of
labellum with a long strap-shaped tip
that is often bent. Column wings lack
the "eyes" found on the other species
of NZ Calochilus.

 More Information
 
Red Beard Orchid
Calochilus robertsonii
Rainbow Mountain - Waiotapu
Photos: Michael Pratt
Flowers October - December
Found on poor clay soils in open areas
amongst scrub. Base of labellum with
short rounded calli. Beard coloured red,
with a short, twisted, strap-shaped tip.
Column wings have distinctive dark "eyes"

 More Information
             
Beak Orchid
Waireia stenopetala
Bald Hill - Longwood Range - Riverton
Photo: Eric Scanlen
Flowers December - February
Found in wet, peaty places at higher
altitudes in the north, & down to sea level
in the south. Has 1 - 3 flowers, each are
green with dull red-brown spots.

 More Information
Horned Orchid
( Paratawhiti, Ikaika )
Orthoceras novae-zeelandiae
Horopito - Mt Ruapehu
Photo: Michael Pratt
Flowers December - March
Has a short floral bract behind the flower,
and a rounded labellum tip. Found
on poor clay soils and beneath scrub.

 More Information
Horned Orchid
( Paratawhiti, Ikaika )
Orthoceras strictum
Wairarapa
Photo: Ian St George
Flowers December - March
Has longer floral bracts above the flower
and a more pointed labellum than is
found on O novae-zeelandiae.
