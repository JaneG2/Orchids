Pink Fingers
Caladenia alata
Syn: Petalochilus alatus
Rainbow Mt - Waiotapu
Photo: Michael Pratt
Flowers August - November
A slender species with pink, white, or
mauve flowers. Labellum mid-lobe is
orange and sharply curled backward.

 More Information
Mauve Fingers
Caladenia minor
Waikumete - Auckland
Photo: Allan Ducker
Flowers September - November
Found mainly in the kauri zone north
of Auckland, but also Wairarapa and
Nelson. Flowers crimson to magenta.


 More Information
Small Pink Fingers
Caladenia pusilla
Syn: Petalochilus pusillus
Papawiri Hill - Te Paki
Photo: Eric Scanlen
Flowers October - November
A small pink flowered species found
in scrub and open clay areas from
Wellington northward. Very slender.

Caladenia variegata
Syn: Petalochilus variegatus
Okoia - Wanganui
Photo: Michael Pratt

Found in scrubland and light forests
at scattered localities. Pale pink with

Caladenia bartlettii
Te Hapua Road - Waitiki Landing
Photo: Eric Scanlen

Found along tracksides and amongst

Red Fingers
Caladenia "red stem"
Syn: Petalochilus "red stem"
Puffer Track - Kaitoke
Photo: Eric Scanlen
Flowers November - January
Resembles Caladenia aff. chlorostyla,
but distinguished by the distinctive
dark red-brown stem.

Bronze Fingers
Caladenia atradenia
Syn: Stegostyla atradenia
Waikumete - Auckland
Photo: Allan Ducker
Flowers September - December
An uncommon & sparse species found
under light forest & scrub with poor soils.
Labellum mid-lobe dark maroon edged
with long marginal calli. Has a slender,
wiry stem covered in short hairs.

 More Information
White Fingers
Caladenia lyallii
Syn: Stegostyla lyallii
Horopito - Mt Ruapehu
Photo: Michael Pratt
Flowers November - January
Found in high altitude scrub and
grasslands in the north, and also
in beech forests in the south. White
flowers with red bars on the labellum
side-lobes, lamina and inner column.

 More Information
White Fingers
Caladenia alpina
Syn: Stegostyla alpina
Arthur's Pass
Photo: Kathleen Shepherd
Flowers November - January
Found in high altitude scrub and
grasslands in the South Island.
Similar in appearance to C lyallii,
but the flowers are larger, with broad
oval sepals and petals.

 More Information
White Fingers
Caladenia "Bacon Creek"
Syn: Stegostyla "Bacon Creek"
Nelson Lakes
Photo: Georgina Upson
Flowers November - December
Known only from mature kanuka scrub
in the Nelson area. 1 to 2 flowers,
usually white but sometimes pale pink.
Dorsal sepal stands almost erect in fully
open flowers and the tip recurves.
