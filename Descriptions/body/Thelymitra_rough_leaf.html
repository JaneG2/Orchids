Plant: leaf upright, rough to the touch.

Flowers: mauve pink with rounded petals and sepals. Column white, top rolled over and opening forwards, topped with a broad yellow band as it arches forward, with two teeth on each side at the lower, forward edge of the yellow band. Column arms with dense white cilia.

Flowering: October.

Habitat: lowland, scrub on clays. Northland.

Conservation: Data defiient, local, scattered.

Notes: seldom opening. A similar plant was found at Shag Point, Otago.

Key features: leaf rough, column erect, scarcely rounded over to a broad, yellow margin, with a pair of short, broad teeth.
