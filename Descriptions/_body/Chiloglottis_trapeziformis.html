Chiloglottis trapeziformis

Plant: Reddish or green flower and stem; to 10cm tall with leaf margins distinctly wavy.

Flowers: to 20 mm across with a pale column and dark, discrete area of warty calli at labellum midlobe, not reaching tip.

Flowering: September.

Habitat: known naturally only from pine forests on the Horowhenua coast but transplanted to Iwitahi.

Conservation: vagrant, from Australia.

Notes: distinguished from C. valida by a bundle of calli surrounded by smaller calli. Appears to be setting seed in NZ.

Key features: flower tinged maroon; mass of large, dark maroon calli on the midlobe.
