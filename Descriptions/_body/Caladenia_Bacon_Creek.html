Caladenia "Bacon Creek"

Plant: tall, medium sized to 25 cm with 1-2 white to pale pink flowers. Leaf, linear about 12cm x 4mm.

Flowers: labellum barred red with 2 rows of yellow topped calli that extend to tip of the midlobe, often with extra partial rows scattered along the midlobe. Midlobe strongly curled with long marginal calli. Sepals and petals externally red-glanded.

Flowering: November- December

Habitat: Prefers moist open areas under manuka scrub with good light. Inland Nelson.

Conservation: localised, taxonomically indeterminate.

Notes: So far only reported from the Nelson region. Plants are quite variable and suggest a hybrid or hybrids between C. lyallii and C. chlorostyla or one of the pink caladenias.

Key features: flower size, calli on the midlobe surface and long marginal calli.
