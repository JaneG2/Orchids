Caladenia lyallii

Plant: 5 to 20cm tall. Leaf broad to 1 cm wide and typically brownish green.

Flowers: 1-2 large, white flowers, 15–25mm across, with variably red barred labellum and 2–6 rows of yellow topped calli extending to the tip of the midlobe which has irregular marginal calli. Petals oblong and pointed, dorsal sepal wider than the labellum and hooded over it.

Flowering: November to January.

Habitat: lowland to alpine tussock, wetlands and scrub. Central North is southwards.

Conservation: locally quite common; not threatened.

Notes: An extremely variable species difficult to separate from the Tasmanian C. alpina.

Key features: large flower size, rather broad brownish leaf and hooded dorsal sepal.
