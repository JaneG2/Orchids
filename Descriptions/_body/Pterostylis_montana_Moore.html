Pterostylis montana sensu Moore

Plant: Up to 30cm tall. Has an overall bronze/green colour. Leaves 2 - 6 narrow, erect leaves up to 15cm long by 1 cm wide, but often smaller.

Flowers: 20 - 30mm tall. Green to bronze with translucent white stripes. Flower is somewhat square in shape, with a short blunt dorsal sepal and short, flat lateral sepals with tips usually curled forwards. Labellum tip twisted to one side. Stigma heart-shaped or globular and prominent.

Flowering: November to December.

Habitat: forest, scrub, grassland, wetlands. Waikato southwards, widespread local.

Conservation: Not Threatened.

Notes: There is debate as to which species is the true Pterostylis montana. This is the species described by Lucy Moore in Flora of NZ Volume 2.

Key features: lateral sepals flat and often curved forwards, rarely as tall as the dorsal sepal.
