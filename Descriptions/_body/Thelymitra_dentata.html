Thelymitra xdentata

Plant: robust, to 40cm tall;

Flowers: up to six white, pink or blue, heavily striped, 15mm diameter. Column tall, top rounded, with a slightly rolled yellow tip and a warty, reddish band at the back. Column arms bent inward with tooth-like lobes towards the bases, cilia yellow grading to brown with age.

Flowering: November to January.

Habitat: scrub, pakihi, damp clay banks.

Conservation: widespread but never abundant, usually just a few plants, not threatened.

Notes: one of the few taxa with striped flowers, easily distinguished by the arched, yellow tipped top to the column. A sterile hybrid (backcross) between T. longifolia and T. pulchella.

Key features: striped flowers, column top rounded, arms with dense yellow cilia.
