Orthoceras novae-zeelandiae

Plant: up to 70cm tall, with several long, stiff, pointed, channelled leaves, shorter than stem.

Flowers: 2â€“12 green to maroon. Lateral sepals like thin horns, upright to diverging horizontally. Broad labellum bends forwards, 3-lobed, tip rounded. Floral bract short.

Flowering: November to January.

Habitat: lowland: dry open banks and grassland. South to Nelson and Marlborough

Conservation: often locally common, not threatened.

Notes: colour and floral bract length variable among plants in the same population.

Key features: several basal leaves and hooded flower with long, stiff, lateral sepals.
